BRIAN CULBERTSON


Brian Culbertson is known for his unique brand of genre-crossing contemporary jazz, R&B and funk. Brian began developing his musical talent at age 8, playing piano. He quickly learned other instruments and by age twelve Brian was playing drums, trombone, bass and euphonium. In interviews, Brian passionately tells how he was inspired by the iconic R&B-jazz-pop artists of the 1970s like Earth, Wind & Fire, Tower of Power, Chicago, David Sandborn and others. He started composing original music for his seventh grade piano recital and has not stopped since! Brian has over 30 Billboard No. 1 singles and an extensive catalogue of 20 albums, most of which have topped the Billboard contemporary jazz charts.

Brian came onto the music scene in 1994 with his first ablum, Long Night Out. The demo was recorded in his college apartment and featured himself playing most of the instruments. Mesa/Bluemoon Recordings produced the final album and launched his career with a six-album contract. Over the years, Brian has collaborated with many artist and won numerous awards from some of the smooth jazz industry's most influencial organizations.

His awards include:

  • 2012 Soul Train Award nomination for Best Jazz Artist
  • 2011 Entertainer of the Year at the Oasis Smooth Jazz Awards
  • 2010 International Artist of the Year at the Canadian Smooth Jazz Awards
  • 2004 All That Jazz Award at the American Society of Young Musicians Awards
  • 2001 Keyboardist of the Year at the Oasis Smooth Jazz Awards

Michelle

Brian is married to Michelle Culbertson, also known by her stage name Micaela Haley. They met at his first concert in 1994 and married in 1997. Michelle contributed background vocals on Brian's earlier albums and is the feature vocalist on Brian's Christmas album, A Soulful Christmas.

In 2006, Michelle and Brian released a touching collection of classic hymns titled Be Still My Soul. Michelle is the feature vocalsit, with piano and arrangements by Brian and cello by David Low.
